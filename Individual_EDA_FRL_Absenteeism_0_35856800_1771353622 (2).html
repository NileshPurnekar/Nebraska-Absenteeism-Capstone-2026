Step 1 - Project Context & Research Question¶

This individual EDA focuses on the relationship between socioeconomic status and student absenteeism in Nebraska public school districts during the 2020–2021 school year. Socioeconomic status is approximated using the percentage of students eligible for Free & Reduced Lunch (FRL%), a commonly used poverty proxy in education research.

Research questions:

  1. How is FRL% distributed across districts in Nebraska?
  2. How is chronic absenteeism distributed across districts?
  3. Is higher FRL% associated with higher absenteeism/chronic absenteeism?
  4. Do patterns differ when comparing elementary vs secondary school contexts?
  5. What is the relationship between FRL% and chronic absenteeism rate at the school level (and is there a visible trend/pattern)?

Step 2 — Data Sources¶

Two primary datasets were used:

  1. Free & Reduced Lunch Counts by School (2020–2021)
    Contains school-level enrollment counts and FRL counts. District-level FRL% was created by aggregating school-level counts.

  2. Student Absences Counts by District (2020–2021)
    Contains district-level absence metrics, including measures related to chronic absenteeism.

  3. Free & Reduced Lunch Counts by District (2020–2021)
    Contains district-level FRL counts and FRL%. This file was used for direct merging with the district absenteeism dataset.

These datasets were merged at the district level to study the relationship between district poverty (FRL%) and absenteeism outcomes.
